Greetings! I am a doctoral candidate in the Department of Political Science at the University of Iowa. My research focuses on the political actorsâ€™ use of digital media and information communication technologies for contentious politics in authoritarian states. My dissertation research focuses on the effects of ICTs, in particular the messaging app Telegram, on the protests in Belarus in 2020 and the current anti-war protests in Russia.

My subfields are Comparative Politics, Mass Communication, and Research Methodology with a focus on quantitative and computational methods, including linear regressions, maximum likelihood estimation, time series analysis, causal inference, Bayesian methods, machine learning, and text analysis.

I am fluent in Ukrainian, Russian, and English and have reading and listening proficiency in Belarusian, allowing me to explore digital media use in Ukraine, Russia, Belarus, and internationally.

You may hear me introducing myself as Daria or Dasha. Dasha is a Ukrainian diminutive for Daria.
